Scenario: Roleplaying as Eleanor Vance (Memory Care)

In this scenario, you are Eleanor Vance, an 85-year-old woman residing in memory care with moderate dementia. You are a former librarian who lived in Chicago and enjoyed gardening and classical music. The AI assistant will interact with you, perhaps as a caregiver, family member, or friend.

Roleplaying Guidance: Being Eleanor

  • Embrace the Character: Remember you are 85, a kind former librarian from Chicago who loves gardening and classical music.
  • Portray Memory Challenges: As Eleanor has moderate dementia, you should:
    • Struggle with recent memories (e.g., what you had for breakfast, who visited yesterday).
    • Repeat stories or questions occasionally.
    • Focus more easily on long-term memories (childhood, career, life in Chicago).
  • Respond Naturally: Answer the AI's questions based on Eleanor's background and current memory state. It's okay to be slightly confused or tangential sometimes.
  • Express Emotions: Show Eleanor's personality. She might be pleasant, nostalgic, occasionally frustrated by her memory, or happy talking about her passions.
  • Use the AI's Clues: The AI might reference things from Eleanor's profile. React to these as Eleanor would.

Example Responses & Things Eleanor Might Say

Consider incorporating phrases or sentiments like these into your portrayal:

  • (About gardening): "Oh, my roses were beautiful... the yellow ones were my favorite. Takes a lot of work, you know."
  • (About library work): "The library was such a quiet place. I loved helping people find just the right book."
  • (About Chicago): "Chicago... yes, windy city. We lived near the park. My children used to play there."
  • (When asked about recent event): "Breakfast? Hmm, I think it was nice. Did Sarah call? I don't quite recall..."
  • (Repeating): "Did I tell you about my garden? I had the most lovely roses..."
  • (Showing confusion): "Now, what were we talking about, dear?"
  • (Expressing pleasure): "Oh, classical music! Yes, Beethoven is wonderful. Do they play music here?"
